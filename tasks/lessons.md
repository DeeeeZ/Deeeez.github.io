# Lessons

## 2026-07-08 — Spec copy is a facts ceiling, not a script
- Correction: user called the shipped copy "cringy AI written", "too fucking wordy", "written like a Resume" — even though most of it was verbatim from his own rebuild spec.
- Lesson: the Facts Registry caps WHAT can be claimed; it does not mean long spec paragraphs should ship as-is. Compress everything to short, client-facing lines. Nobody cares about career chronology ("invoicing officer, then a team of 10") — cut biography, keep proof.
- Rule: before shipping copy, reread every paragraph asking "would a busy finance director say 'get to the point'?" Max ~2 short sentences per block.

## 2026-07-08 — This user's design bar is "premium dark with a pulse"
- Correction: v1 (dark hero + corporate light bands) got "the design fucking sucks". v2 full-dark with glass, grain, glow and an animated recon panel landed the brief.
- Rule: for this user, default to full-dark, one hot accent, big display type, one signature animated moment. Light "corporate" bands read as dull to him. Use the advanced-frontend-skill checklist when he asks for premium.

## 2026-07-08 — Meta-cleverness about the page is not a feature
- Correction: the footer "Page tie-out" (sections posting as read, SIGNED OFF stamp) got "wtf is that", "mind boggling not mind blowing" and an immediate removal order.
- Lesson: subject-derived structure means the SITE's content is shaped like the subject's materials (recon panel, tie-out table = his actual work). A widget about the visitor's own reading behavior is self-referential cleverness; it impresses the builder, confuses the client, and sells nothing.
- Rule: every element must answer "what does this tell a finance director about hiring him?" If the answer is about the page itself, cut it. Quiet supporting details (cause line, spine fill) passed; the meta-widget did not.

## 2026-07-08 — "Dark + glass + glow" is itself AI slop
- Correction: v2 still read as template ("unslop the slop"). Glassmorphism blur, gradient text, radial glow blobs, uniform card grids, decorative `01 ·` kickers, and the Space Grotesk + Inter + JetBrains Mono stack are the AI-default dark look.
- What worked (v3): derive structure from the SUBJECT — ledger spine down the margin, spec-sheet rows with real codes, case studies as a tie-out table, status-semantic color (green matched / amber pending), sharp corners, flat surfaces. Distinctiveness comes from subject-derived structure, not effects.
- Rule: before shipping a design, ask "would I produce this exact page for any other client in this industry?" If yes, find the subject's own materials (here: ledgers, statements, recon runs) and rebuild the structure from them.
